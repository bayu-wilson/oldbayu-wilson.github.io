Lyman beta

Radiation emitted from supermassive black holes in the early universe (quasars) is absorbed by hydrogen in the intergalactic medium (IGM). As shown in Figure 1, hydrogen absorbs light at certain resonant frequencies. Statistics of Lyman-alpha absorption (the most studied resonant frequency of hydrogen) have been used to constrain the Universe’s initial conditions and particle constituents (such as the amount and mass of the dark matter) and the temperature of intergalactic gas (which constrains how the universe was heated). To further improve these constraints, we use another Lyman series transition (Lyman-alpha. Due to the smaller absorption cross-section of Lyman-alpha this line’s absorption probes higher gas densities than measured with the Lyman-alpha transition, providing complementary constraints on the temperature and on cosmological parameters to existing constraints from Lyman-alpha absorption.
